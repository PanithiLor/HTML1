Ornamental fish

Ornamental fish are species of fish that are kept primarily for their aesthetic value rather than for practical purposes like food or work. They are often bred and selected for their striking colors, patterns, and shapes. These fish are commonly kept in home aquariums, garden ponds, and public displays.

Examples of ornamental fish include:
Goldfish: Known for their bright orange color and variety of shapes and fin types.
Koi: A type of ornamental carp prized for their vibrant colors and patterns.
Betta Fish: Popular for their vivid colors and flowing fins, often kept in smaller tanks.
Guppies: Small, colorful fish that are easy to care for and breed.
Neon Tetras: Recognizable by their bright blue and red stripes.
Ornamental fish come in a wide range of species, each with its own unique appearance and care requirements.
